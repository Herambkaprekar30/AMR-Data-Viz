AMR : The Silent Pandemic

What is AMR ?

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) is when microorganisms like bacteria, viruses, and fungi no longer respond to medicines used to treat infections. This makes infections harder to treat and increases the risk of spreading disease. Human activity, such as misusing or overusing antimicrobials, accelerates the emergence and spread of AMR.

India has witnessed a sharp rise in antibiotic sales, driven by increasing infections, easy availability, and self-medication. Commonly used antibiotics like azithromycin and amoxicillin are widely purchased, often without prescriptions. While antibiotics are essential in treating bacterial infections, their misuse—such as incomplete courses, overuse for viral illnesses, and indiscriminate self-medication—has led to a surge in antimicrobial resistance (AMR). Experts warn that this trend could render many life-saving drugs ineffective in the future, posing a severe public health challenge.

In 2019 alone, antibiotic-resistant illnesses are thought to have directly killed about 300,000 people in India, including newborns.

Antimicrobial resistance is expected to cause 2 million deaths in India alone by 2050.
